How We Work in our services

01

Visual identity graphic design

Graphic designers for visual identity collaborate with brand stakeholders to create assets such as logos, typography, color schemes, and image collections that represent the soul of a business. In addition to traditional business cards and corporate stationery, designers frequently create a series of visual brand guidelines (style guides) that explain suggested practises and demonstrate visual branding applied across many mediums. These rules are critical for maintaining brand consistency in future deployments.

02

Marketing and advertising graphic design

Marketing designers collaborate with business owners, directors, managers, or marketing specialists to develop various marketing assets. They can work independently or as part of a creative or internal team, specializing in specific mediums like car wraps or magazine adverts, or creating collateral for print, digital, and other media. While initially focused on print, this design discipline has expanded to encompass a wide range of digital assets, particularly those used in content marketing and digital advertising.

03

User interface graphic design

The user interface (UI) serves as the gateway for users to interact with devices or applications. UI design involves crafting interfaces that prioritize usability and deliver a seamless user experience. It encompasses all elements of user interaction, including the screen, keyboard, and mouse. UI design primarily focuses on enhancing the visual aspects of user experience, encompassing on-screen graphic components such as buttons, menus, micro-interactions, and other features. A UI designer aims to achieve a harmonious blend of aesthetic appeal and technological functionality.

04

Motion graphic design

Motion graphics designers begin with storyboards and use animation, video, and traditional art to bring their visions to life. Marketing, coding, and 3D modeling skills might be useful advantages depending on the industry.

05

Art and illustration for graphic design

While the terms graphic design, graphic art, and illustration are frequently used interchangeably, they are not the same thing. Graphic designers construct compositions to communicate messages and solve issues, whereas graphic artists and illustrators create unique artwork. Fine art, decorative items, and narrative illustrations are all examples of their work. Although graphic art and illustration are not types of graphic design in and of themselves, they are regularly utilised in commercial situations within the area of graphic design. As a result, discussing one discipline without mentioning the others is challenging.

06

Publication graphic design

Publication designers work with editors and publishers to create layouts that include well-curated typography as well as complementing artwork such as illustrations, graphics, and photographs. These designers can work as freelancers, as part of a creative agency, or as in-house designers for a publishing firm.
